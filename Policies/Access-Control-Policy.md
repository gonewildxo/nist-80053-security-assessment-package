# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Access Control Policy is to establish requirements for granting, managing, reviewing, and revoking access to Able Behavioral Health information systems, applications, and data.

This policy is intended to:

- Protect organizational information assets
- Prevent unauthorized access
- Support HIPAA compliance requirements
- Enforce least privilege principles
- Reduce cybersecurity risk

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All employees
- Contractors
- Temporary workers
- Third-party vendors
- Managed service providers
- All systems and devices connected to organizational resources

This includes:

- Microsoft 365 services
- Electronic Health Record (EHR) systems
- VPN access
- Workstations and laptops
- Cloud applications
- Shared drives and file storage systems

---

# 3. Policy Statement

Access to organizational systems and information shall be:

- Authorized
- Limited to business necessity
- Managed through approved procedures
- Regularly reviewed
- Revoked when no longer required

All users are responsible for protecting organizational credentials and complying with access management requirements.

---

# 4. Access Control Principles

Able Behavioral Health follows the following access control principles:

## Least Privilege

Users shall receive the minimum level of access required to perform job responsibilities.

## Need-to-Know

Access to sensitive information is limited to personnel with a legitimate business requirement.

## Separation of Duties

Critical functions shall be distributed among multiple individuals where feasible to reduce fraud and misuse risks.

---

# 5. User Account Management

## Account Provisioning

User accounts shall only be created after:

- HR authorization
- Management approval
- Completion of onboarding procedures

Each user shall be assigned a unique account identifier.

Shared accounts are prohibited unless explicitly approved for operational necessity.

---

## Account Reviews

User access reviews shall occur:

- Quarterly for standard accounts
- Monthly for privileged accounts
- Immediately following role changes

Inactive or unnecessary accounts shall be disabled promptly.

---

## Account Termination

Access must be revoked immediately upon:

- Employee termination
- Contractor separation
- Role change requiring removal of access

The Human Resources department shall notify IT immediately of personnel changes impacting system access.

---

# 6. Authentication Requirements

## Password Standards

Passwords must:

- Be at least 12 characters long
- Contain uppercase and lowercase letters
- Include numbers and special characters
- Not be reused across systems
- Not contain easily guessed information

Default passwords must be changed immediately.

---

## Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

MFA is required for:

- VPN access
- Administrative accounts
- Cloud services
- Remote access systems
- Microsoft 365 accounts

---

# 7. Privileged Access Management

Administrative privileges shall be restricted to authorized IT personnel only.

Privileged accounts must:

- Be uniquely assigned
- Use MFA
- Be monitored through logging
- Be reviewed regularly

Administrative accounts shall not be used for normal day-to-day activities.

---

# 8. Remote Access Controls

Remote access to organizational systems is permitted only through approved VPN solutions.

Remote access requirements include:

- MFA enforcement
- Encrypted communications
- Approved organizational devices where possible
- Endpoint security protection

Unauthorized remote access methods are prohibited.

---

# 9. Access to HIPAA-Regulated Data

Access to Protected Health Information (PHI) shall be restricted to authorized workforce members with legitimate business needs.

Users accessing PHI must:

- Complete HIPAA training
- Follow confidentiality requirements
- Use approved systems only
- Report unauthorized disclosures immediately

---

# 10. Monitoring and Logging

The organization shall monitor and log:

- Authentication attempts
- Privileged account activity
- Failed login attempts
- Remote access sessions
- Access to sensitive systems

Logs shall be reviewed regularly by authorized personnel.

---

# 11. Physical Access Controls

Physical access to sensitive areas and systems shall be restricted using:

- Badge access systems
- Locked server rooms
- Visitor sign-in procedures
- Surveillance systems where appropriate

Unauthorized individuals shall not access restricted areas.

---

# 12. Third-Party Access

Third-party vendors requiring system access must:

- Obtain management approval
- Sign confidentiality agreements
- Use MFA where applicable
- Follow organizational security requirements

Vendor access shall be reviewed regularly and disabled when no longer needed.

---

# 13. User Responsibilities

All users are responsible for:

- Protecting passwords and credentials
- Logging off unattended systems
- Reporting suspicious activity
- Following security policies
- Preventing unauthorized access

Users shall not:

- Share passwords
- Use another user's account
- Circumvent access controls
- Install unauthorized software

---

# 14. Security Violations

Violations of this policy may result in:

- Access suspension
- Disciplinary action
- Employment termination
- Legal action where applicable

---

# 15. Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy must:

- Be documented
- Include business justification
- Receive executive approval
- Undergo risk review
