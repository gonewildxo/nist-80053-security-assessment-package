# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Password Policy is to establish requirements for the creation, management, protection, and use of passwords and authentication credentials within Able Behavioral Health.

This policy is intended to:

- Protect organizational systems and sensitive information
- Reduce unauthorized access risks
- Support HIPAA compliance requirements
- Improve overall cybersecurity posture

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All employees
- Contractors
- Temporary staff
- Vendors with organizational access
- Managed service providers

This policy applies to all systems including:

- Microsoft 365
- Electronic Health Record (EHR) systems
- VPN access
- Windows workstations and laptops
- Cloud applications
- Administrative systems

---

# 3. Policy Statement

All users must create and maintain secure passwords that protect organizational resources and sensitive information from unauthorized access.

Passwords and authentication credentials are considered confidential and shall not be shared, disclosed, or stored insecurely.

---

# 4. Password Requirements

## Minimum Standards

Passwords must:

- Be at least 12 characters in length
- Contain uppercase letters
- Contain lowercase letters
- Include numbers
- Include special characters where supported
- Avoid dictionary words when possible

---

## Prohibited Password Practices

Users shall not use:

- Easily guessed passwords
- Personal information
- Birthdates
- Employee IDs
- Sequential characters
- Reused organizational passwords
- Shared passwords

Examples of prohibited passwords include:

- Password123
- Welcome1
- CompanyName2026

---

# 5. Password Expiration and Reuse

## Password Changes

Passwords shall be changed:

- Immediately if compromise is suspected
- Upon administrative request
- Following security incidents
- During account recovery processes

Routine password expiration is not required unless risk conditions warrant additional controls.

---

## Password History

Systems shall prevent reuse of the previous 10 passwords where technically feasible.

---

# 6. Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

MFA is required for:

- VPN access
- Administrative accounts
- Microsoft 365 accounts
- Cloud applications containing sensitive data
- Remote access systems

Approved MFA methods include:

- Authenticator applications
- Hardware security tokens
- Push notifications

SMS-based MFA should be avoided where stronger options are available.

---

# 7. Password Storage

Passwords shall:

- Never be stored in plaintext
- Be encrypted or hashed using approved security methods
- Not be written on paper in unsecured areas
- Not be stored in unsecured spreadsheets or documents

Approved password managers may be used when authorized by IT.

---

# 8. Account Lockout Controls

Systems shall implement account lockout protections to reduce brute-force attacks.

Minimum standards include:

- Account lockout after multiple failed login attempts
- Temporary lockout duration
- Administrative review for repeated failures

---

# 9. Administrative and Privileged Accounts

Privileged accounts must:

- Use unique passwords
- Use MFA
- Not be shared among users
- Be monitored through logging and auditing

Administrative accounts shall not be used for daily email or internet browsing activities.

---

# 10. Service Accounts

Service account passwords shall:

- Be securely managed
- Be changed periodically where feasible
- Be restricted to required services only
- Not be used for interactive logins

---

# 11. User Responsibilities

Users are responsible for:

- Maintaining password confidentiality
- Reporting suspected compromise immediately
- Using approved authentication methods
- Following organizational security requirements

Users shall not:

- Share passwords
- Store passwords insecurely
- Reuse organizational passwords for personal accounts
- Circumvent authentication controls

---

# 12. Monitoring and Enforcement

The organization reserves the right to:

- Monitor authentication activity
- Audit compliance with this policy
- Investigate suspicious login behavior
- Disable accounts presenting security risks

---

# 13. Incident Reporting

Suspected credential compromise must be reported immediately to:

- IT Department
- Compliance Department
- Security personnel

Examples include:

- Phishing attacks
- Unauthorized login notifications
- Lost devices containing credentials
- Credential exposure incidents

---

# 14. Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy require:

- Written business justification
- Risk assessment review
- Executive approval
- Documentation of compensating controls
