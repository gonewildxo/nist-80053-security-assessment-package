# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Acceptable Use Policy is to establish rules and expectations for the appropriate use of Able Behavioral Health information systems, devices, networks, applications, and data.

This policy is intended to:

- Protect organizational information assets
- Support patient privacy and HIPAA compliance
- Reduce cybersecurity risks
- Promote responsible technology usage
- Prevent unauthorized or inappropriate activities

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All employees
- Contractors
- Vendors
- Temporary personnel
- Interns
- Third-party service providers with organizational access

This policy applies to all organizational technology resources including:

- Computers and laptops
- Mobile devices
- Microsoft 365 services
- Email systems
- VPN access
- Internet access
- Cloud applications
- Electronic Health Record (EHR) systems
- Network infrastructure

---

# 3. Policy Statement

Organizational technology resources are provided primarily for authorized business purposes.

Users are expected to use systems responsibly, ethically, securely, and in compliance with organizational policies and applicable laws and regulations.

---

# 4. Authorized Use

Authorized use includes activities necessary to support:

- Patient care operations
- Administrative functions
- Business communications
- Compliance activities
- Approved remote work
- Training and professional responsibilities

Limited personal use may be permitted if it:

- Does not interfere with business operations
- Does not consume excessive resources
- Does not violate organizational policies
- Does not create security risks

---

# 5. Prohibited Activities

Users shall not engage in activities including, but not limited to:

- Unauthorized access attempts
- Sharing passwords or credentials
- Installing unauthorized software
- Circumventing security controls
- Accessing offensive or illegal content
- Downloading pirated software or media
- Using systems for personal business ventures
- Sending phishing or malicious communications
- Introducing malware or unauthorized devices
- Disabling security software
- Excessive personal internet use
- Unauthorized disclosure of sensitive information

---

# 6. Protection of Sensitive Information

Users handling sensitive information must:

- Follow data classification requirements
- Protect HIPAA-regulated PHI
- Use approved systems only
- Encrypt sensitive data when required
- Avoid unauthorized sharing of information

Sensitive information shall not be:

- Shared through personal email accounts
- Stored on unauthorized devices
- Posted publicly
- Discussed with unauthorized individuals

---

# 7. Email and Communication Usage

Organizational email systems shall be used responsibly.

Users shall not:

- Send inappropriate or offensive content
- Open suspicious attachments or links
- Forward chain messages or spam
- Use email for harassment or discrimination
- Conduct unauthorized external communications

Users should report suspicious emails immediately.

---

# 8. Internet Usage

Internet access is provided for legitimate business purposes.

The following activities are prohibited:

- Accessing illegal content
- Downloading unauthorized software
- Streaming excessive non-business media
- Visiting malicious or unsafe websites
- Engaging in activities that create cybersecurity risks

The organization reserves the right to monitor internet usage.

---

# 9. Remote Access and VPN Usage

Remote access to organizational systems must occur through approved secure methods.

Users accessing systems remotely must:

- Use approved VPN solutions
- Follow MFA requirements
- Maintain secure home network practices
- Prevent unauthorized individuals from viewing sensitive information

Public or unsecured Wi-Fi networks should be avoided when accessing organizational systems.

---

# 10. Mobile Device Usage

Users accessing organizational resources through mobile devices must:

- Use device security protections
- Report lost or stolen devices immediately
- Avoid storing sensitive information unnecessarily
- Follow organizational mobile security requirements

The organization may require mobile device management (MDM) controls.

---

# 11. Monitoring and Privacy

Organizational systems are subject to monitoring and logging.

Users should have no expectation of personal privacy when using organizational systems or devices.

Monitoring activities may include:

- Email monitoring
- Internet activity logging
- Authentication logging
- File access monitoring
- Security event analysis

Monitoring is conducted to support security, compliance, and operational requirements.

---

# 12. Software Installation

Only authorized software may be installed on organizational systems.

Software installations must:

- Be approved by IT personnel
- Comply with licensing requirements
- Meet security standards

Users shall not install unauthorized applications or tools.

---

# 13. Security Incident Reporting

Users must immediately report:

- Phishing emails
- Malware infections
- Lost or stolen devices
- Unauthorized access
- Data exposure incidents
- Suspicious system activity

Reports should be directed to:

- IT Department
- Compliance Department
- Management personnel

---

# 14. User Responsibilities

All users are responsible for:

- Following organizational policies
- Protecting passwords and credentials
- Maintaining physical security of devices
- Reporting security concerns
- Using systems responsibly

Users must complete required cybersecurity and HIPAA training.

---

# 15. Violations and Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in:

- Access restriction or suspension
- Disciplinary action
- Employment termination
- Legal or regulatory consequences

The organization reserves the right to investigate suspected violations.

---

# 16. Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy require:

- Written business justification
- Risk review
- Executive approval
- Documentation of compensating controls

# 18. Compliance

Failure to comply with this policy may negatively impact the organization's security posture and regulatory obligations, including HIPAA compliance requirements.
