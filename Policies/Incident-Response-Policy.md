# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Incident Response Policy is to establish a structured approach for identifying, reporting, responding to, containing, and recovering from cybersecurity incidents affecting Able Behavioral Health systems and data.

This policy is intended to:

- Minimize operational disruption
- Protect sensitive organizational and patient data
- Support HIPAA compliance requirements
- Reduce the impact of cybersecurity incidents
- Improve organizational response readiness

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All employees
- Contractors
- Vendors with system access
- Managed service providers
- All organizational systems and devices

This includes:

- Microsoft 365 services
- Electronic Health Record (EHR) systems
- VPN infrastructure
- Endpoints and laptops
- Cloud applications
- Network devices
- Backup systems

---

# 3. Policy Statement

Able Behavioral Health shall maintain a formal incident response capability to identify, manage, investigate, and recover from cybersecurity incidents in a timely and effective manner.

All workforce members are required to report suspected security incidents immediately.

---

# 4. Definition of a Security Incident

A security incident is any event that may compromise:

- Confidentiality
- Integrity
- Availability
- Privacy
- Security of organizational systems or data

Examples include:

- Malware infections
- Ransomware attacks
- Phishing attacks
- Unauthorized access
- Lost or stolen devices
- Data breaches
- Denial-of-service attacks
- Insider misuse
- Credential compromise

---

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|

# 6. Incident Response Lifecycle

Able Behavioral Health follows a structured incident response lifecycle based on industry best practices.

---

# Phase 1 — Preparation

Preparation activities include:

- Security awareness training
- Incident response procedures
- Vulnerability management
- Security monitoring tools
- Backup and recovery processes
- Tabletop exercises
- Access control enforcement

---

# Phase 2 — Identification

Potential incidents may be identified through:

- Security alerts
- User reports
- Log monitoring
- Antivirus detections
- Vulnerability scanning
- Third-party notifications

All suspected incidents must be documented and evaluated promptly.

---

# Phase 3 — Containment

Containment actions may include:

- Isolating affected devices
- Disabling compromised accounts
- Blocking malicious IP addresses
- Removing systems from the network
- Restricting unauthorized access

Containment actions should minimize operational disruption while reducing risk exposure.

---

# Phase 4 — Eradication

Eradication activities may include:

- Malware removal
- Credential resets
- System patching
- Vulnerability remediation
- Removal of unauthorized software
- Elimination of attacker persistence mechanisms

---

# Phase 5 — Recovery

Recovery activities include:

- Restoring systems from backups
- Returning systems to production
- Validating system functionality
- Monitoring for recurring malicious activity
- Confirming remediation effectiveness

---

# Phase 6 — Lessons Learned

Following significant incidents, the organization shall conduct a post-incident review to:

- Identify root causes
- Evaluate response effectiveness
- Improve procedures
- Update policies and controls
- Document corrective actions

---

# 7. Incident Severity Levels

| Severity | Description |
|---|---|
| Low | Minimal operational impact |
| Medium | Limited business disruption or unauthorized activity |
| High | Significant operational impact or sensitive data exposure |
| Critical | Major system outage, ransomware, or large-scale PHI breach |

---

# 8. Reporting Requirements

All workforce members must report suspected incidents immediately to:

- IT Department
- Compliance Department
- Management personnel

Examples requiring immediate reporting include:

- Suspicious emails
- Unauthorized login alerts
- Lost devices
- Malware detections
- Data exposure incidents

Failure to report incidents promptly may increase organizational risk.

---

# 9. HIPAA Breach Response

Security incidents involving Protected Health Information (PHI) shall be evaluated according to HIPAA breach notification requirements.

The organization shall:

- Assess potential exposure
- Document findings
- Determine notification obligations
- Coordinate legal and compliance review
- Notify affected parties where required

---

# 10. Evidence Preservation

During investigations, personnel shall:

- Preserve logs and system evidence
- Avoid altering compromised systems unnecessarily
- Document investigative actions
- Maintain chain-of-custody records where applicable

---

# 11. Communication Procedures

Incident communications shall be coordinated through authorized personnel.

External communications involving incidents may require:

- Executive approval
- Legal review
- Compliance review

Unauthorized disclosure of incident information is prohibited.

---

# 12. Security Monitoring

The organization shall maintain security monitoring capabilities including:

- Endpoint protection
- Log collection
- Authentication monitoring
- Vulnerability scanning
- Alert management

Monitoring tools help support early incident detection.

---

# 13. Backup and Recovery

Critical systems shall be backed up regularly to support recovery operations.

Backup procedures include:

- Daily backups
- Secure storage
- Periodic restoration testing
- Offsite retention where applicable

---

# 14. Training and Awareness

All personnel shall complete:

- Annual cybersecurity awareness training
- HIPAA training
- Phishing awareness education
- Incident reporting instruction

Incident response personnel may receive additional specialized training.

---

# 15. Testing and Exercises

Incident response procedures shall be tested periodically through:

- Tabletop exercises
- Simulated phishing campaigns
- Disaster recovery testing
- Technical response validation

Testing results shall be documented and reviewed.

---

# 16. Third-Party Coordination

Third-party vendors and service providers involved in organizational operations must:

- Report security incidents promptly
- Cooperate during investigations
- Follow contractual security obligations
- Support remediation efforts where applicable

---

# 17. Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy require:

- Written justification
- Risk assessment review
- Executive approval
- Documentation of compensating controls
