# Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish a standardized framework for classifying, handling, storing, transmitting, and disposing of organizational data at Able Behavioral Health.

Proper data classification helps protect sensitive information, supports regulatory compliance requirements, and reduces organizational risk related to unauthorized disclosure, modification, or destruction of data.

---

# Scope

This policy applies to:

- All employees
- Contractors
- Third-party vendors
- Interns
- Temporary personnel
- All organizational systems and devices

This policy applies to all forms of data, including:

- Electronic data
- Printed documents
- Email communications
- Cloud-stored information
- Backup media
- Mobile device data

---

# Data Classification Levels

Able Behavioral Health classifies information into four categories based on sensitivity and business impact.

---

# 1. Public

## Definition

Information approved for public disclosure that presents minimal risk to the organization if shared externally.

## Examples

- Public website content
- Marketing materials
- Job postings
- Public press releases
- Publicly available policies

## Security Requirements

- No special handling requirements
- May be shared publicly with approval
- Integrity protections should still be maintained

---

# 2. Internal

## Definition

Information intended for internal organizational use only that is not considered confidential or regulated.

## Examples

- Internal procedures
- Staff directories
- Internal announcements
- Meeting schedules
- Training documentation

## Security Requirements

- Accessible only to authorized personnel
- Should not be publicly disclosed
- Stored on approved organizational systems

---

# 3. Confidential

## Definition

Sensitive business or employee information that could negatively impact operations, privacy, or reputation if disclosed without authorization.

## Examples

- Employee records
- Financial reports
- Vendor contracts
- Internal audit reports
- Security procedures
- Non-public operational data

## Security Requirements

- Access restricted based on business need
- Encryption required during transmission
- Secure storage required
- Sharing limited to authorized personnel

---

# 4. Restricted

## Definition

Highly sensitive regulated information requiring the highest level of protection due to legal, regulatory, or operational requirements.

## Examples

- Protected Health Information (PHI)
- Electronic Health Records (EHR)
- Patient treatment records
- Social Security numbers
- Authentication credentials
- Security incident investigation data

## Security Requirements

- Strict least-privilege access controls
- Multi-factor authentication required
- Encryption required at rest and in transit
- Continuous monitoring and logging required
- Secure disposal procedures required
- HIPAA safeguards must be enforced

---

# Data Handling Requirements

| Classification | Access Control | Encryption Required | External Sharing Allowed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Public | Minimal | No | Yes |
| Internal | Authorized Personnel | Recommended | Limited |
| Confidential | Restricted Access | Yes | Approval Required |
| Restricted | Strictly Restricted | Mandatory | Highly Restricted |

---

# Data Owners

Department managers and system owners are responsible for:

- Proper classification of data
- Reviewing access permissions
- Ensuring compliance with this policy
- Reporting improper handling of sensitive information

---

# Employee Responsibilities

All workforce members must:

- Handle data according to classification requirements
- Report suspected data exposure incidents
- Use approved systems for storing sensitive information
- Follow all organizational security policies

Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

---

# Data Retention and Disposal

Sensitive data must be retained and securely disposed of according to:

- HIPAA requirements
- Organizational retention schedules
- Legal and regulatory obligations

Approved disposal methods include:

- Secure shredding
- Cryptographic wiping
- Certified electronic destruction

---

# Exceptions

Any exception to this policy must be:

- Documented
- Approved by executive leadership
- Reviewed by the Compliance Department
